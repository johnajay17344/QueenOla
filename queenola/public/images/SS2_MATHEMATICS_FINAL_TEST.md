## QUESTIONS

**1.** A ship sails from port A on a bearing of 075°. What is the bearing of A from the ship?
- a. 075°
- b. 105°
- c. 255°
- d. 285°

**2.** Express the three-figure bearing 150° as a compass bearing.
- a. N30°E
- b. S30°E
- c. N60°W
- d. S60°E

**3.** Two towns, X and Y, are such that Y is on a bearing of 230° from X. If the distance between X and Y is 15 km, find the bearing of X from Y.
- a. 050°
- b. 130°
- c. 310°
- d. 040°

**4.** A cyclist rides 10 km due North, then 6 km due East. What is the bearing of the final point from the starting point? (to the nearest degree)
- a. 031°
- b. 059°
- c. 121°
- d. 149°

**5.** The angle of elevation of the top of a tower from a point 50 m away from its base is 30°. Find the height of the tower. (tan 30° = 0.5774)
- a. 14.4 m
- b. 25.0 m
- c. 28.9 m
- d. 86.6 m

**6.** A ladder leans against a wall making an angle of elevation of 60° with the ground. If the foot of the ladder is 4 m from the wall, find the length of the ladder. (cos 60° = 0.5)
- a. 2 m
- b. 4 m
- c. 6.93 m
- d. 8 m

**7.** The angle of depression of a point on the ground from the top of a building 45 m high is 35°. Find the distance from the point to the foot of the building. (tan 35° = 0.7002)
- a. 31.5 m
- b. 45.0 m
- c. 64.3 m
- d. 78.5 m

**8.** A boy 1.5 m tall observes the top of a tree at an angle of elevation of 40°. If the boy stands 20 m from the tree, find the height of the tree. (tan 40° = 0.8391)
- a. 15.3 m
- b. 16.8 m
- c. 18.3 m
- d. 20.0 m

**9.** Two points A and B are on the same horizontal level as the foot of a tower. The angle of elevation of the top of the tower from A is 45° and from B is 30°, where A is closer to the tower. If AB = 20 m, find the height of the tower. (Use tan 45° = 1, tan 30° = 0.5774)
- a. 20.0 m
- b. 27.3 m
- c. 34.6 m
- d. 40.0 m

**10.** The angle of elevation of the top of a pole from a point on the ground is 50°. If the height of the pole is 30 m, find the distance from the point to the foot of the pole. (tan 50° = 1.1918)
- a. 15.6 m
- b. 25.2 m
- c. 30.0 m
- d. 35.8 m

**11.** In a frequency distribution, the class 21-25 has a class boundary of:
- a. 20.5 - 25.5
- b. 21.5 - 25.5
- c. 20.5 - 24.5
- d. 21 - 25

**12.** The class width of the class interval 30-39 is:
- a. 9
- b. 9.5
- c. 10
- d. 10.5

**13.** Given the frequency table below:

| Class | 1-5 | 6-10 | 11-15 | 16-20 |
|-------|-----|------|-------|-------|
| Frequency | 3 | 7 | 9 | 5 |

Find the cumulative frequency of the class 11-15.
- a. 9
- b. 10
- c. 19
- d. 24

**14.** In a frequency distribution table, if the cumulative frequencies are 4, 10, 18, 25, 30, find the frequency of the 3rd class.
- a. 4
- b. 6
- c. 8
- d. 18

**15.** The cumulative frequency curve is also known as:
- a. Histogram
- b. Ogive
- c. Bar chart
- d. Pie chart

**16.** A fair die is rolled once. What is the probability of obtaining a number greater than 4?
- a. 1/6
- b. 1/3
- c. 1/2
- d. 2/3

**17.** A bag contains 5 red balls, 3 blue balls, and 2 green balls. What is the probability of picking a blue ball at random?
- a. 1/5
- b. 3/10
- c. 1/2
- d. 3/5

**18.** Two events A and B are said to be mutually exclusive if:
- a. P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)
- b. P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)
- c. P(A and B) = 1
- d. P(A) = P(B)

**19.** A coin is tossed twice. What is the probability of getting two heads?
- a. 1/8
- b. 1/4
- c. 1/2
- d. 3/4

**20.** If P(A) = 0.4, and A and B are complementary events, find P(B).
- a. 0.4
- b. 0.5
- c. 0.6
- d. 1.4
